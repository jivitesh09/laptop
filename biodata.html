MISSILE MAN OF INDIA

Name : Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam

Date of birth : 15 October 1931

Place of birth : Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu


EDUCATION 👇

  • Schooling:  Attended Schwartz Higher Secondary School in Ramanathapuram, Tamil Nadu.

  • Undergraduation:  Graduated in Physics from St. Joseph's College, Tiruchirappalli, in 1954.

  • Aerospace Engineering:  Studied aerospace engineering at the Madras Institute of Technology (MIT), graduating in 1960.

Career in Science and Technology 👇

  • ISRO and DRDO:  Kalam played a crucial role in India's space and defense programs. He was involved with the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) and the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO).

  • Missile Development:   Known for his work on the development of ballistic missile technology, Kalam was a key figure in the development of the Agni and Prithvi missiles.

  • He was the Chief Project Coordinator during the Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998, which established India as a nuclear power.


  • He served India as 11th President from the year 2002-2007.

Awards and Honors 👇

  • Bharat Ratna:  1999

  • Padma Bhushan  1981

  • Padma Vibhushan  1990
